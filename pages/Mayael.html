Executive Summary

Herculoids is a classic Naya "Big Stompy" deck built around cheat-into-play engines and overwhelming combat steps. The deck aims to leverage Mayael the Anima's activated ability to dig for massive, high-impact creatures with power 5 or greater, bypassing their high mana costs and putting them directly onto the battlefield at instant speed. The primary mechanical interactions revolve around creature-based keywords like hexproof (Archetype of Endurance), uncounterable setups (Spellbreaker Behemoth), and massive power scaling features (Ghalta, Stampede Tyrant, Quartzwood Crasher). It wins strictly via the combat phase by dropping lethal, trample-enabled threats, accelerating damage via force multipliers like Atarka, World Render and Twinflame Tyrant, or turning non-creature permanents into damage via Where Ancients Tread.

1. Synergy & Engine
6.0 / 10

The mechanical synergy between Mayael the Anima and the creature base is structurally sound, featuring 41 creatures, with an overwhelming majority meeting the crucial "Power 5 or greater" restriction.

  • High-velocity engine pieces like Monstrous Vortex and Where Ancients Tread reward you with immediate cascading value or direct damage whenever a creature enters the battlefield.
  • Elvish Piper and Loot, Exuberant Explorer act as excellent secondary backup cheat mechanics when Mayael is taxed or unavailable.
  • Internal Anti-Synergy Danger: 1x Manaplasm scales based on casting cost, which non-bos entirely with Mayael’s activation mechanics. Furthermore, top-deck manipulation is completely non-existent, leaving you entirely at the mercy of blind variance when activating Mayael.
2. Interaction & Board Control
4.5 / 10

The deck handles problematic artifacts and enchantments beautifully via utility threats integrated directly into the creature payload.

  • Acidic Slime, Terastodon, Kogla and Yidaro, and Woodfall Primus provide premium removal strapped to high-power bodies.
  • Instant-speed stack interaction and targeted creature removal are dangerously low, relying almost exclusively on Path to Exile and Return to Dust.
  • The board wipe suite is highly risky: Austere Command is wonderfully modal, but Planar Cleansing is a devastating self-inflicted wound that wipes out your own commander, your mana rocks, and value engines like Where Ancients Tread. Settle the Wreckage provides a solid defensive blowout trick, but the deck lacks ways to survive opposing faster combo wins.
3. Consistency & Tutors
3.0 / 10

This is the deck’s most glaring vulnerability, severely capping its performance envelope in high-power casual metas.

  • There are zero creature tutors (Worldly Tutor, Chord of Calling) and zero top-deck manipulation engines (Sylvan Library, Sensei's Divining Top, Scroll Rack).
  • Without these, Mayael’s 6-mana activation is a pure gamble that can easily whiff if the top 5 cards contain only lands, small utility creatures, or sorceries.
  • Card draw is also dangerously light, relying heavily on situational engines like Drumhunter and Garruk's Uprising / Monstrous Vortex style templates. If Mayael is removed twice, the deck frequently stalls out with a hand full of uncastable 7-drops and 8-drops.
4. Speed & Mana Base
5.0 / 10

The land count is stable, but the composition and execution are noticeably slow for an assertive Naya deck.

  • The deck runs too many tap-lands that kill early-game velocity (Arctic Flats, Highland Weald, Kazandu Refuge, Rugged Highlands, Timber Gorge, Wind-Scarred Crag).
  • While the basic land base uses a clean Snow-Covered suite, it doesn't currently run any payoffs for it (like Into the North).
  • The ramp package leans heavily on fragile creature options (Hulking Raptor, Arbor Colossus) rather than low-cost, resilient land ramp (Three Visits, Farseek, Nature's Lore). This creates a structural bottleneck where you cannot reliably establish Mayael before turn 4 or 5.
5. Win Conditions & Closeness to "Infinite"
5.5 / 10

The deck is an honest, combat-focused machine with 0% infinity metrics. There are no infinite combat loops or instant-win combos present.

  • It relies completely on explosive combat finishers. Atarka, World Render provides devastating double strike to your dragons, while Twinflame Tyrant acts as a lethal damage doubler.
  • Quartzwood Crasher creates massive dinosaur tokens out of nowhere, and Siege Behemoth allows your army to bypass blockers entirely to deal combat damage directly to opponents' faces.
  • Ghalta, Stampede Tyrant offers a magnificent hand-dumping win condition if you happen to draw your big creatures rather than flipping them with Mayael.
