Executive Summary

This deck is a classic Simic Sea Monster/Top-Deck Manipulation engine that leverages Kenessos, Priest of Thassa to cheat massive, high-mana-value Krakens, Leviathans, Octopuses, and Serpents (KLOS) into play at instant speed. The deck uses a blend of scry engines, top-deck filters, and cost-reducers to set up high-value activations for just {1}{G/U}. It wins by establishing overwhelming card advantage and battlefield presence through massive combat steps, locking down opponents' boards with tap effects, and utilizing unblockable sea monster synergies to bypass blockers entirely.

Synergy & Engine

8.5 / 10
  • Top-Deck Setups: Cards like Cream of the Crop, Retreat to Coralhelm, Elrond, Master of Healing, Galadriel of Lothlórien, and Planetarium of Wan Shi Tong ensure that the top card of the library is almost always guaranteed to be a massive sea monster before pulling the trigger on Kenessos. Cantrips like Brainstorm, Brainsurge, Opt, and Preordain pull double duty as early-game consistency tools and late-game top-deck stackers.
  • Activation Discounts: The inclusion of Training Grounds and Biomancer's Familiar drops Kenessos’s activation cost down to a single green or blue mana ({G} or {U}), allowing you to spin the wheel multiple times per turn cycle at instant speed.
  • Value Engines: Elemental Bond, Garruk's Uprising, Kiora, Behemoth Beckoner, and Aesi, Tyrant of Gyre Strait turn every cheated sea monster into immediate card draw, ensuring your hand stays fueled with interaction even as your board explodes.

Interaction & Board Control

7.0 / 10
  • Instant-Speed Protection: Elite interaction like Fierce Guardianship, Swan Song, Counterspell, Heroic Intervention, and Beast Within provide high-tempo answers to board wipes and targeted removal aiming to disrupt your setups.
  • Thematic Sweepers: The deck utilizes incredibly potent one-sided board resets. Cyclonic Rift, Whelming Wave, and Slinn Voda, the Rising Deep clear away opposing aggressive boards and token strategies while leaving your massive deep-sea threats completely untouched to swing for lethal.
  • Repeatable Control: Hullbreaker Horror and Koma, Cosmos Serpent act as interactive engines on their own, letting you control the stack and lock down key opposing permanents turn after turn.

Consistency & Tutors

5.5 / 10
  • Filtering Dominance: The deck compensates for a lack of hard tutors by using extreme card selection. Brainstorm combined with fetch-likes or scry effects acts as a pseudo-tutor. Quest for Ula's Temple provides an alternate, highly consistent cheat engine if Kenessos faces heavy targeting.
  • Recursion: Noxious Revival provides crucial safety, allowing you to put a countered or destroyed sea monster right back on top of your library to immediately cheat out again with a Kenessos activation response.
  • Tutor Deficit: The lack of cards like Worldly Tutor, Mystical Tutor, or Green Sun's Zenith means the deck relies on drawing into its setup engines naturally, which can occasionally lead to slower openings if the top-deck filters are removed.

Speed & Mana Base

6.5 / 10
  • The Land Base: Breeding Pool, Hinterland Harbor, Dreamroot Cascade, and Yavimaya, Cradle of Growth provide excellent fixing. However, the inclusion of 18 basic Islands creates a slight color imbalance that can occasionally bottleneck green mana requirements early on.
  • Ramp Suite: Core ramp pieces like Sol Ring, Arcane Signet, Talisman of Curiosity, and Nature's Lore are strong, alongside creature-based ramp like Arixmethes, Slumbering Isle.
  • Velocity Warning: If Kenessos is repeatedly removed, the deck's average mana value is incredibly high. Without an active cheat engine or Wilderness Reclamation, hard-casting multiple 7+ CMC sea monsters back-to-back can leave the deck vulnerable to faster, lower-to-the-ground archetypes.

Win Conditions & Closeness to "Infinite"

5.0 / 10
  • Combat Finishers: The primary win condition is overwhelming combat damage. Spawning Kraken builds a terrifying army of 9/9 tokens exponentially fast. Serpent of Yawning Depths makes your entire sea monster army completely unblockable against non-island decks, ending games in a single combat step.
  • Stompy Support: Return of the Wildspeaker and Rishkar's Expertise can draw 7 to 10+ cards instantly, often hitting a massive threat that pumps your team or drops an extra win condition straight to the battlefield.
  • Closeness to Infinite (0.0 / 10): There are no infinite mana combos (e.g., Pili-Pala/Grand Architect) or infinite turn loops. The deck respects the casual boundary completely, winning via raw, unadulterated combat scaling and tempo dominance.
