Executive Summary

This deck is an engine-driven Boros Midrange/Aristocrats strategy centered around micro-value zone changes. Quintorius, Field Historian acts as a localized payoff engine, translating the exile or return of cards from the graveyard into immediate 3/2 Spirit creature tokens. The deck operates by filtering through its library with looting and rummaging mechanisms, filling the graveyard, and using a suite of reanimation, flashback, and escape packages to trigger Quintorius multiple times per turn. Win conditions rely on token-swarming wide boards, augmented by anthem effects like Balefire Liege and Caretaker's Talent, or crushing over-the-top finishers such as Moonshaker Cavalry.

Synergy & Engine
8.5 / 10

The synergy core of this deck is exceptionally well-constructed, functioning as a high-velocity value machine when undisturbed.

  • The Engine Nodes: Card pairs like Containment Construct and Conspiracy Theorist transform standard discarding (from card selection or hand-size rules) into active impulsive card draw while cleanly generating Spirit tokens. Currency Converter and Bag of Holding store discarded assets, doubling down on exile triggers.
  • Graveyard Extraction: Serra Paragon, Sevinne's Reclamation, and Sun Titan pull permanents straight from the yard, triggering Quintorius on the exit while re-establishing field presence. Meanwhile, self-exiling cards like Squee, the Immortal and Anger provide repeatable or highly impactful utility when shifted across zones.
  • Token Amplification: Caretaker's Talent is a massive standout here, quickly leveling up to clone tokens and draw extra cards, while Staff of the Storyteller converts token creation into sustainable card advantage.
Interaction & Board Control
6.0 / 10

The interaction suite features premier spot removal but lacks instant-speed reactive breadth and flexible wipes.

  • Targeted Point Removal: The inclusion of Swords to Plowshares, Path to Exile, Rip Apart, and Skyclave Apparition ensures that the deck can remove problematic singular threats, blockers, or problematic stax pieces cleanly.
  • Board Wipes & Protection: Tragic Arrogance provides an asymmetric board wipe that can leave your best engine pieces untouched while dismantling opposing boards. Defensively, Guardian of Faith and Selfless Spirit offer excellent protection against opposing sweepers.
  • The Vulnerability: The deck leans heavily toward proactive sorcery-speed interaction (Lorehold Charm, Rip Apart). It lacks the deeper reactive interaction (such as Tibalt's Trickery or Deflecting Swat) necessary to stop fast combo decks or protect its own graveyard from sudden, instant-speed exile effects like Bojuka Bog or Soul-Guide Lantern.
Consistency & Tutors
4.5 / 10

In accordance with its casual design philosophy, the deck avoids high-powered tutor systems, relying instead on raw card selection and velocity.

  • Filtering & Velocity: Cards like Faithless Looting, Seize the Spoils, and Millikin help cycle through the library while fueling the graveyard. Tocasia's Welcome and Idol of Oblivion keep the hand full as 3/2 Spirits enter the battlefield.
  • The Consistency Gap: The deck contains zero traditional tutors (e.g., Enlightened Tutor, Gamble). This means you cannot reliably search for critical engine enablers like Containment Construct or your protection spells. You are entirely at the mercy of the top cards of your library, making games variance-heavy.
Speed & Mana Base
6.5 / 10

The mana base and ramp package are functional for a midrange strategy but contain several high-utility inclusions that slow down raw tempo.

  • Ramp & Fixing: Classic pieces like Sol Ring, Arcane Signet, and Fellwar Stone provide early acceleration. Archaeomancer's Map ensures land drops keep pace with green decks, while Bitterthorn, Nissa's Animus yields repeatable land ramp on an attack trigger.
  • Utility vs. Tempo Lands: Utilizing Emeria, the Sky Ruin, Lotus Field, and Mistveil Plains offers superb late-game utility and graveyard manipulation. However, running multiple enters-the-battlefield-tapped or conditional sources like Furycalm Snarl, Sacred Peaks, and Terramorphic Expanse noticeably slows down the deck's ability to curve out aggressively in the early turns.
Win Conditions & Closeness to Infinite
7.0 / 10

The deck strictly adheres to the "No Infinite Combos" restriction, relying entirely on combat damage through token generation and anthems.

  • Combat Finishers: Moonshaker Cavalry serves as the primary craterhoof-style win condition, turning a board of 3/2 Spirits into an immediate, lethal airborne squad. Balefire Liege pumps the team while turning red and white spells into direct burn and life swing. Augusta, Order Returned maximizes combat steps by untapping attackers and boosting power.
  • Aristocrats/Burn Burn-out: Vanguard of the Restless and Hofri Ghostforge ensure that even when your creatures die, they leave behind massive value or extra bodies, grinding down opponents over prolonged games.
