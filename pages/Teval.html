Executive Summary

Boys from the Yard is a high-synergy Sultai midrange deck engineered around a resilient, self-milling graveyard engine and land-recursion value loops. The deck utilizes Teval, the Balanced Scale as a mechanical pivot point to balance resource states, converting high-volume self-mill into explosive board states via mass graveyard recursion, landfall triggers, and compounding token generation. It wins by deploying massive threat vectors like Lord of Extinction and Avenger of Zendikar, crushing opponents via combat damage or draining the table using Jarad, Golgari Lich Lord flings.

Synergy & Engine 8.5 / 10

The deck boasts a robust engine where almost every card serves a dual mechanical purpose. Mill engines such as Stitcher's Supplier, Hedron Crab, and Blossoming Tortoise rapidly feed the graveyard, transforming it into a second hand. Lands-matter packages driven by Life from the Loam, Six, and Conduit of Worlds turn discarded or sacrificed lands into repeatable card advantage and mana acceleration.

  • Dropping a land triggers Tireless Provisioner and Lotus Cobra (via Summon: Titan), while fueling Drownyard Temple or Afterlife from the Loam setups.
  • Meren of Clan Nel Toth and Phyrexian Reclamation extract high-value creatures from the bin, ensuring that key synergy pieces like Adrix and Nev, Twincasters or Gisa, the Hellraiser can be re-established immediately after a board wipe.
Interaction & Board Control 7.0 / 10

Your interaction suite is lean, fast, and targeted. It features premier, high-velocity answers like Cyclonic Rift, Toxic Deluge, and Beast Within to answer problematic, non-land permanents or clear blockers. Free or highly efficient stack protection like Fierce Guardianship, Swan Song, and Heroic Intervention provides a security blanket for your big engine turns.

  • The deck is slightly vulnerable to aggressive flyers and fast, spell-based combo decks if you don't draw your limited stack interaction early.
  • The inclusion of Lethal Scheme is an excellent synergistic choice, allowing you to tap your token armies to kill threats while looting more lands or reanimation targets into the graveyard.
Consistency & Tutors 6.0 / 10

Consistency is primarily achieved through raw card velocity, structural self-mill, and continuous draw engines rather than explicit tutoring. Cards like River Kelpie, Uro, Titan of Nature's Wrath, and Lier, Disciple of the Drowned keep your hand full and give you access to spells casting out of the graveyard.

  • Because you aren't running traditional black demonic tutors, finding specific answers or exact win-conditions relies on digging deep into your library or milling half your deck.
  • This satisfies high-power casual ethics but means finding an exact card like Haywire Mite in a pinch requires setup.
Speed & Mana Base 8.0 / 10

The mana base is exceptionally well-tuned for a three-color deck utilizing a lands-matter theme. Breeding Pool, Overgrown Tomb, and Watery Grave provide searchable targets for Nature's Lore and Harrow, while tech lands like Cephalid Coliseum and Crypt of Agadeem offer uncounterable utility and massive late-game mana generation.

  • The acceleration is explosive because of cards like Open the Way, Springbloom Druid, and Blossoming Tortoise (which discounts activated land abilities like Memorial to Folly or Spymaster's Vault).
  • This allows the deck to routinely out-pace standard casual tables, hitting 6–8 mana by turn 4 or 5.
Win Conditions & Closeness to "Infinite" 7.0 / 10

True to your strict No Infinite Combos rule, the deck wins through overwhelming, fair combat states or massive, single-turn bursts of non-infinite damage. The deck approaches the high-velocity line of high-power casual play without crossing into infinite territory, keeping game states highly dynamic and dependent on combat math or life totals.

  • Overwhelming the board with tokens via Avenger of Zendikar or Bonny Pall, Clearcutter, amplified by Adrix and Nev, Twincasters.
  • Resolving a massive Living Death to swap an empty board for a graveyard full of high-value creatures.
  • Growing a colossal Lord of Extinction or Multani, Yavimaya's Avatar and instantly killing the table by sacrificing them to Jarad, Golgari Lich Lord.
