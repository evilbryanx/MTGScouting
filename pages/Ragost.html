Executive Summary: Bisquey Business is an artifact-aristocrat and theft-sacrifice engine deck built entirely around utilizing Ragost, Deft Gastronaut as a high-velocity utility piece. The mechanical core relies on taking control of opponents' key artifacts and creatures via temporary theft spells, utilizing them for maximum combat value or tapping utility, and then sacrificing them to value engines before they can be returned. The deck converts these sacrificed cardboard assets into massive piles of Food, Clues, and Treasures. It leverages token-multiplier synergies to drown opponents in card advantage and chip damage, culminating in explosive non-infinite combat steps, massive artifact drain effects, or alt-win conditions like Hellkite Tyrant.

Strategic Pillar Deep-Dive

1. Synergy & Engine
8.5 / 10

The deck functions as a highly synergistic, well-oiled engine room. Ragost, Deft Gastronaut acts as a brilliant payoff for sacrificing artifacts, turning your utility pieces into life gain, card draw, or scry velocity.

  • Token Multipliers: The inclusion of Academy Manufactor alongside Nuka-Cola Vending Machine and Oltec Matterweaver forms the absolute golden triangle of the deck. Generating a Food token instantly cascades into a Treasure and a Clue, triggering Crime Novelist to generate massive pools of red mana.
  • Sacrifice Outlets & Value Loops: Witch's Oven, Clock of Omens, and Goblin Engineer provide instantaneous, instant-speed sacrifice opportunities. This guarantees that any creature borrowed with Bloody Betrayal, Mass Mutiny, or Mob Rule is ground up into culinary components before the end step. Ichor Wellspring, Prized Statue, and Myr Retriever ensure that your graveyard loops remain highly active, recycling your engine components seamlessly.
2. Interaction & Board Control
7.0 / 10

The interactive suite leverages Boros’s premium removal alongside specialized theft-based board disruption.

  • Targeted Removal: Swords to Plowshares, Path to Exile, and Dispatch represent the absolute gold standard for instant-speed, spot creature removal. Dispatch is almost always a hard 1-mana exile spell given how easily this deck achieves Metalcraft. Wear // Tear offers clean, modular removal for problematic enchantments or opposing stax pieces.
  • Theft as Disruption: Spells like Seize the Spotlight and Mob Verdict serve dual purposes. They strip critical blocking lines or engine pieces from your opponents while feeding your own material requirements.
  • Board Wipes: Farewell, The Battle of Bywater, and Slaughter the Strong are perfectly asymmetric. Slaughter the Strong and The Battle of Bywater consistently spare your utility creatures (like Astrid Peth, Rosie Cotton of South Lane, and Ragost) while completely dismantling massive commander-centric boards or tall green stompy strategies.
3. Consistency & Tutors
4.0 / 10

This is where the deck intentionally throttles its power level to remain within a casual ecosystem.

  • Tutor Limitations: The deck consciously avoids a heavy tutor suite. Goblin Engineer is the primary specialized tutor, acting as an exceptional tool to place Academy Manufactor, Clock of Omens, or Nuka-Cola Vending Machine directly into the graveyard for immediate reanimation. Outside of this, the deck relies purely on raw velocity.
  • Draw Engines: What the deck lacks in direct tutoring, it attempts to offset through explosive draw engines. The Gaffer triggers incredibly easily due to Ragost's culinary output. Smuggler's Share and Monologue Tax capitalize heavily on opponents' game actions to keep your hand full and your board state populated with Treasures. However, without cards like Enlightened Tutor or Gambit, assembling specific three-card engine pairs relies heavily on natural card draw.
4. Speed & Mana Base
6.5 / 10

The mana base is highly functional but contains notable avenues for structural speed optimizations.

  • Acceleration: The ramp package is solid for a mid-power casual deck. Sol Ring, Arcane Signet, Boros Signet, and Talisman of Conviction provide the standard, necessary turn-2 acceleration. Archaeomancer's Map provides excellent land-catchup utility.
  • Land Assessment: The inclusion of Great Furnace and Ancient Den is highly strategic, providing immediate artifact count for Metalcraft or casual sacrifice fodder. However, the presence of 26 basic lands (13 Plains, 13 Mountain) alongside slower duals like Boros Garrison and Furycalm Snarl noticeably limits the deck’s explosive potential. The color requirements can occasionally stumble on turns 1-3 when attempting to balance early white utility creatures with red-heavy theft spells.
5. Win Conditions & Closeness to "Infinite"
5.5 / 10

The deck respects the user’s explicit No Infinite Combos rule perfectly, relying on high-synergy, overlapping combat states and explosive mass-theft sequences to cross the finish line.

  • Combat Finishers: Virtue of Loyalty provides continuous, incremental team-wide growth, transforming a small army of food tokens or utility creatures into genuine lethal threats. Yuffie, Materia Hunter and Sami, Wildcat Captain capitalize on the massive artifact count to swing for lethal commander-adjacent numbers.
  • Alternative Win States: Hellkite Tyrant acts as an absolute powerhouse alternative win condition. Given how easily Academy Manufactor and Ragost can clog the board with 20+ tokens in a single rotation, a single untap step with Hellkite Tyrant frequently wins the game on the spot.
  • Closeness to Infinite: While cards like Clock of Omens and Academy Manufactor border on loop-heavy territory, the lack of traditional layout multipliers (such as *Ruthless Knave* or *Splinter Twin* effects) guarantees the deck remains entirely within the bounds of a legal, high-power, fair casual decklist.
