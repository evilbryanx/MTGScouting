Executive Summary

Bad Apple is a specialized Enchantress/Goad control deck built around Eriette of the Charmed Apple. The mechanical core relies on attaching Aura enchantments directly to opponents' creatures, which simultaneously soft-removes those threats via Eriette's structural pillow-fort clause ("they can't attack you or planeswalkers you control") while forcing opponents to bludgeon each other. The deck leverages heavy life-drain dynamics during your end step, scaling linearly with the number of Auras you control. Win conditions culminate in massive life-swing drains, high-powered damage amplifiers like Bloodletter of Aclazotz, or critical alpha strikes fueled by extreme power spikes from Ethereal Armor and Sage's Reverie.

Synergy & Engine 8.5 / 10

The deck runs an incredibly cohesive engine that masterfully turns standard aura card-disadvantage on its head. Engines like Light-Paws, Emperor's Voice, Kor Spiritdancer, and Pearl-Ear, Imperial Advisor keep your hand fully fueled. The implementation of Ondu Spiritdancer allows you to clone high-impact Auras, doubling their combat-control and life-drain efficacy. Special utility choices like Codsworth, Handy Helper offer cheap, repeatable flash speed attachment, making reactive aura plays highly viable. Hall of Heliod's Generosity provides excellent resilience against targeted removal or board wipes, establishing a recurring loop of oppressive political locks.

Interaction & Board Control 8.0 / 10

Your removal suite is versatile, operating seamlessly at both instant speed and via recursive permanent-based locks. Standard premium options like Swords to Plowshares, Path to Exile, and Anguished Unmaking handle immediate threats, while Damn and Farewell offer catastrophic reset buttons. The real brilliance lies in your pseudo-removal Auras: Darksteel Mutation strips problematic commander abilities entirely, while Ghoulish Impetus, Martial Impetus, and Parasitic Impetus hijack your opponents' biggest threats, forcing aggressive combat phases elsewhere. Sphere of Safety and Ghostly Prison scale alongside your Auras to create an impenetrable pillow fort.

Consistency & Tutors 8.5 / 10

Consistency is a massive strong point for this build. You are running the premium black tutor package—Demonic Tutor and Vampiric Tutor—alongside elite white aura fetchers including Enlightened Tutor and Open the Armory. These allow you to immediately find silver bullets like Phyresis for an unexpected infection kill, or Serra's Sanctum to unlock astronomical mana production. The land base is exceptionally reliable due to Land Tax ensuring hit land drops, combined with superb filtering from the pathway, shock, and crowd lands.

Speed & Mana Base 7.0 / 10

While your color fixing is incredibly tuned via Godless Shrine, Shattered Sanctum, and Serra's Sanctum, the raw velocity of your mana acceleration lags slightly behind high-power casual standards. Beyond Sword of the Animist and basic rocks, the deck relies heavily on keeping high-cost cards like Bolas's Citadel and Sun Titan in hand without consistent ways to cheat them out early. The average converted mana cost is slightly top-heavy for an Aura deck, meaning an early game disruption of your low-cost engine pieces can occasionally stall your momentum for a turn or two.

Win Conditions & Closeness to Infinite 6.0 / 10

The deck deliberately sacrifices true, clean infinite combos for high-synergy, lethal combat loops. Your primary victory path is slow burn via Eriette of the Charmed Apple, drastically accelerated by Bloodletter of Aclazotz or **Sheoldred, the Apocalypse**. Secondary lethal strategies involve turning an opponent's creature into a sudden win condition via Phyresis or Glistening Oil, or deploying a massive evasive threat utilizing Angelic Destiny alongside Ethereal Armor. While Mikaeus, the Unhallowed offers massive board resilience and combo potential, the deck lacks a dedicated secondary combo piece to abuse it instantly, keeping this build firmly rooted in fair, high-power interactive play.
