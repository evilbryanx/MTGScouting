Executive Summary: This deck is an aristocratic Orzhov "Legends Matter" mid-range engine. It seeks to generate high individual value from legendary creatures like Kambal, Consul of Allocation, Liesa, Shroud of Dusk, and Shadrix Silverquill to bleed opponents out through incremental tax effects and combat steps. The underlying core leverages death-trigger engines like Ratadrabik of Urborg, Teysa, Orzhov Scion, and Elenda, Saint of Dusk to create overwhelming board states when trading pieces or wiping the board. It wins by turning legendary recursion and token creation loops into unstoppable combat damage or death-trigger drains.
Synergy & Engine
8.5 / 10

The synergy here is incredibly high, fueled by the legendary supertype restriction. Ratadrabik of Urborg stands out as the ultimate anchor, turning every dying legendary creature into a non-legendary Zombie copy, completely breaking the legend rule when combined with Moira and Teshar or Zoraline, Cosmos Caller. Additional engines like Relic of Legends and Heroes' Podium transform your legendary density into massive mana acceleration and lethal stat pumps, respectively. Amalia Benavides Aguirre and Karlov of the Ghost Council synergize heavily with your low-level life drain pieces, converting small, passive lifegain triggers into aggressive card filtration and targeted removal.

Interaction & Board Control
7.5 / 10

The deck boasts a robust, versatile removal package that makes it highly interactive. Point-removal spells like Swords to Plowshares, Path to Exile, and Anguished Unmaking handle immediate threats efficiently, while utility pieces like Despark, Fracture, and Stroke of Midnight patch vulnerabilities to non-creature permanents. On the board-wipe side, Farewell and Austere Command offer incredible flexibility, allowing you to sculpt asymmetric board wipes that preserve your legendary enchantments or planeswalkers like Kaya the Inexorable while decimating your opponents' assets.

Consistency & Tutors
4.5 / 10

Consistency is the deck's main bottleneck. It relies entirely on raw draw spells like Night's Whisper, Phyrexian Arena, and reactive draw engines like Trouble in Pairs, Mangara, the Diplomat, and Breena, the Demagogue. Because the list features zero traditional tutors, finding crucial combo-disruptors or specific payoff pieces like Ratadrabik of Urborg depends strictly on top-deck variance. While Zoraline, Cosmos Caller and Sevinne's Reclamation offer excellent graveyard recursion to repeat your threats, the deck can occasionally stall out if its primary draw engines are suppressed.

Speed & Mana Base
6.0 / 10

The mana base is operational but features a few low-tempo lands and an average ramp package. Traditional accelerators like Sol Ring, Arcane Signet, and Talisman of Hierarchy provide standard early turns, while Archaeomancer's Map helps you keep pace against green ramp decks. However, the land package contains several slow entries like Eclipsed Steppe, Shineshadow Snarl, and basic land-dependent utility slots that can cause awkward sequencing in the first three turns. The average converted mana cost is slightly high, requiring a cleaner transition into mid-game mana chunks.

Win Conditions & Closeness to "Infinite"
5.0 / 10

Per your deckbuilding rules, there are zero deterministic infinite loops here. Instead, the deck aims to close out matches using heavy combat engines and value cascades. Primevals' Glorious Rebirth acts as a massive win condition, reanimating an entire graveyard of legendary threats at once. If Heroes' Podium or Deathbringer Liege are on the field, the resulting stat bonuses turn your legendary army into a lethal combat threat. The absence of infinite combos lowers its ceiling against true high-power competitive lists, but it functions perfectly as a dominant, fair mid-range threat.
