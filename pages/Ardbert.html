Executive Strategy Summary

This deck is an optimized Orzhov engine built to grind down opponents via relentless recursive loops. It leverages high-value legendary targets alongside powerful protective assets to manipulate combat dynamics and graveyard states. By intentionally removing infinite combinations, the list maximizes interactive gameplay value, establishing an absolute mid-to-late-game dominance that thrives heavily on attrition.

Synergy & Engine 7.5 / 10

The deck boasts incredibly deep mechanical synergy by pairing legendary-matters structural pieces with highly recursive aristocrat engines. Ratadrabik of Urborg acts as an absolute anchor, turning every dying legendary creature into a non-legendary zombie token copy, allowing you to double-dip on powerful death triggers. Value engines like Teysa, Opulent Oligarch and Zoraline, Cosmos Caller seamlessly keep resources flowing from the yard back to the battlefield while Breena, the Demagogue and Mangara, the Diplomat systematically punish your opponents' actions with massive card-draw advantages.

  • Synergy Loop: Having Ratadrabik of Urborg and Athreos, God of Passage out simultaneously creates an unmanageable psychological choice for your opponents, yielding immediate token rewards while recovering the base creature asset.
Interaction & Board Control 7.0 / 10

The interaction profile is highly adaptable and pinpoint accurate. Standard premier Orzhov instant-speed removal spells including Swords to Plowshares, Path to Exile, Anguished Unmaking, and Despark ensure that any distinct threat can be easily managed. The mass board wipe configuration is equally stellar: Farewell and Austere Command deliver unmatched tactical flexibility, while Promise of Loyalty serves as an exceptional political reset tool that actively leaves your best legendary asset intact.

  • Tactical Warning: The list leans heavily into single-target responses rather than recurring, engine-driven control features. Getting locked into top-decking situations against multiple broad board states can quickly cause interaction velocity to stall.
Consistency & Tutors 4.0 / 10

In adherence to clean high-power casual formats, the deck implements zero traditional black tutors (e.g., Demonic Tutor or Vampiric Tutor). Instead, card velocity is fueled completely by pure card-draw engines like Night's Whisper, Phyrexian Arena, and Trouble in Pairs. While this guarantees high gameplay variance and refreshing natural lines, it naturally caps overall structural consistency math, putting you at the total mercy of your top decks to track down key missing synergy pieces.

Speed & Mana Base 6.5 / 10

The mana base is stable and functional, running key dual-color lands such as Godless Shrine, Isolated Chapel, and Caves of Koilos alongside supportive legend environments like Great Hall of the Citadel. Acceleration utilizes legendary components beautifully, with Relic of Legends performing as a massive mana-producing powerhouse alongside basic staples like Sol Ring and Arcane Signet.

  • The Bottleneck: The list suffers from a heavy concentration of high-cost 4 and 5-drop legendary cards. Paired with a standard land count, initial hands can often feel slow and clunky if initial land drops or early ramp rocks are missed.
Win Conditions & Closeness to "Infinite" 5.5 / 10

Strictly obeying your deckbuilding constraints, there are completely zero infinite combos integrated within the list. Win-states are driven entirely via honest combat steps, high-impact combat pumps, or incremental draining loops. Top-end finishers like Primevals' Glorious Rebirth can completely resurrect an entire graveyard army in a single turn, while Heroes' Podium scales up to turn your legendary board into immense un-blockable threats. Continuous bleed elements like Kambal, Consul of Allocation and Liesa, Shroud of Dusk consistently compress your opponents' life totals over time.
