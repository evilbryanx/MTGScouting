Definitive Playstyle Mechanics & Win Conditions
The deck operates as a Grixis Cheat/Clone Midrange-Combo engine. It leverages copy effects and temporary reanimation abilities, utilizing Obeka, Brute Chronologist to bypass end-step cleanup steps. It wins by amassing an unstoppable, permanent army of cloned heavy-hitters or by assembling infinite token/turn loops.
Strategic Pillar Breakdown
1. Synergy & Engine 9 / 10
Your internal synergy is incredibly tightly wound. You have done a fantastic job including structural redundancy so the deck doesn't crumble if Obeka is heavily targeted.
  • The Clone & Token Keepers: Cards like The Master, Multiplied, Flameshadow Conjuring, Jaxis, the Troublemaker, and Orthion, Hero of Lavabrink create temporary tokens that Obeka makes permanent. The Master, Multiplied is an absolute MVP here—completely overriding the legend rule and myriad sacrifice triggers all on his own.
  • The Graveyard Engine: Araumi of the Dead Tide, Feldon of the Third Path, and Sedris, the Traitor King turn your graveyard into a permanent toolbox. Encore and Unearth triggers go on the stack at the end step; you end the turn, and suddenly those unearthed monsters are yours to keep.
  • Redundancy Checks: Including Sundial of the Infinite and Discontinuity ensures that even if Obeka is stuck in the command zone with a massive tax, your deck can still function perfectly.
  • The Vulnerabilities: A few cards like Avaricious Dragon and Curse of Obsession are highly explosive but can backfire disastrously if Obeka is removed in response to your end step, leaving you with an empty hand.
2. Interaction & Board Control 7 / 10
Your interaction package is versatile, flavor-forward, and packs some excellent asymmetric punches.
  • Asymmetric Wipes & Theft: Curse of the Swine exiles problematic threats while leaving your massive token clones intact. Come Back Wrong and Firbolg Flutist are excellent here—you steal an opponent's best creature, and instead of giving it back or sacrificing it, Obeka lets you keep it forever.
  • Stack Manipulation: Swan Song, Counterspell, and Disallow provide crucial protection. Disallow, Time Stop, and Glorious End double as both interaction for opponents' turns and emergency safety valves to end your own turn if Obeka is tapped down.
  • Room for Growth: The spot removal package is a bit light on instant-speed, low-mana flexible options. Swapping a high-mana sorcery removal for something like Terminate, Bedevil, or An Offer You Can't Refuse would push this score higher.
3. Consistency & Tutors 6.5 / 10
The deck has massive raw card-drawing potential, though it relies heavily on making engines stick rather than precision tutoring.
  • Jaw-Dropping Card Advantage: Ideas Unbound is a mind-blowing UU spell in this deck; Obeka lets you skip the end-step discard clause entirely. Day's Undoing acts as a fresh wheel of 7 cards without passing the turn to your opponents if cast on your turn and ended immediately.
  • Filtering: Brainstorm and Mulldrifter (especially when cloned or reanimated) keep the gears turning.
  • Consistency Bottleneck: This deck runs almost zero traditional tutors (like Demonic Tutor or Entomb). While this keeps the deck incredibly fun and high-variance for casual tables, it hurts the pure statistical consistency of finding your payoff pieces exactly when you need them.
4. Speed & Mana Base 7 / 10
Your mana base is functional and your ramp is decent, but your curve leans slightly towards the heavier mid-to-late game.
  • The Speed Packages: Final Fortune, Last Chance, and Alchemist's Gambit are your ultimate speed enables. For 2 mana, you take an extra turn, and Obeka clears the "you lose the game" trigger. This allows for incredibly explosive, early-game wins.
  • Mana Synergies: Lotus Field is a spectacular inclusion. If you play it and respond to its enter-the-battlefield trigger by ending your turn with Obeka, you get a black-border Black Lotus land without sacrificing any lands.
  • Optimization Goals: You have quite a few lands that enter the battlefield tapped under certain conditions (like Frostboil Snarl, Choked Estuary, and Smoldering Marsh). In a three-color deck wanting to cast strict costs like UU (Ideas Unbound) and RRR (Kiki-Jiki), a few more untapped pain lands or crowd lands would maximize efficiency.
5. Win Conditions & Closeness to "Infinite" 8.5 / 10
This deck closes out games beautifully, shifting effortlessly between massive combat steps and definitive infinite loops.
  • The Infinite Loops: You are running Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker, which opens up easy infinite win states. While you aren't running his traditional one-card partner (Pestermite/Deceiver Exarch), combining Kiki-Jiki with copy-multipliers or cloning heavy-hitters with Blade of Selves (Myriad) quickly results in a table-shattering lethal stampede.
  • The Value Overrun: Even without going infinite, making 5 permanent copies of Hellkite Courser, Zara, Renegade Recruiter, or Elturel Survivors with Orthion or The Master, Multiplied creates a combat step that no casual pod can realistically survive.
