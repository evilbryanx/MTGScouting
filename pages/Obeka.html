Executive Summary

This deck operates as a high-velocity Grixis "Cheat & Cheat Later" midrange-tempo build utilizing Obeka, Brute Chronologist as a structural loophole engine. By activating Obeka during your own clean-up step, you permanently exile delayed trigger templates such as "at the beginning of the next end step." This allows you to permanently keep hyper-efficient temporary clones, reanimated massive threats, and un-truncated extra turns. Win conditions revolve around overwhelming board presence generated by un-sacrificed tokens via The Master, Multiplied, Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker, or lethal extra-turn combat loops via Final Fortune and Alchemist's Gambit.

Synergy & Engine
9.5 / 10

The absolute crown jewel of this build. The synergy density here is pristine. Cards like Feldon of the Third Path, Mimic Vat, Araumi of the Dead Tide, and Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker turn from high-value incremental tools into backbreaking permanent value generation engines under Obeka's gaze. Special mention goes to The Master, Multiplied, which acts as a secondary, passive redundant piece that prevents your tokens from ever needing an Obeka activation to survive. Wizards of Thay grants your extra-turn sorceries flash, turning your main phase liabilities into sweeping defensive or offensive instant-speed plays.

Interaction & Board Control
7.0 / 10

A highly respectable, compact interaction suite. You sport elite premier free protection spells like Fierce Guardianship and Deflecting Swat to shield Obeka, which is mandatory since the deck's structural engine chokes without her online. Board wipes like Cyclonic Rift and Blasphemous Act provide standard emergency resets. However, the spot removal is slightly lacking in card velocity, relying heavily on temporary theft/cloning effects like Zara, Renegade Recruiter and Firbolg Flutist. While stealing and keeping threats with Obeka is hilarious, it leaves you vulnerable to fast, non-creature combo engines.

Consistency & Tutors
4.5 / 10

This is the deck's primary bottleneck. You are running zero traditional black tutors (Demonic Tutor, Vampiric Tutor), meaning you are completely reliant on raw card draw engines like Mulldrifter, Ideas Unbound, and Painful Truths. While Brainstorm and Pull from Tomorrow help sift through cards, a bad opening hand or a well-timed board wipe can stall your engine out entirely. Without tutors, finding your definitive protection or your specific missing piece (a cloner vs. a clone target) is entirely left to variance.

Speed & Mana Base
7.5 / 10

The mana base is exceptionally solid, featuring premier Grixis duals (Watery Grave, Blood Crypt, Steam Vents) and efficient check/filter structures. Fast mana is bolstered by Sol Ring, Arcane Signet, and Lotus Field (which Obeka completely breaks by exiling the sacrificial trigger). The average mana value is slightly top-heavy due to a high density of 5+ drop reanimation targets and clone engines. This causes some clunky early-game turns if your low-cost rocks or draw spells aren't found immediately.

Win Conditions & Closeness to "Infinite"
6.5 / 10

Per your restriction, this deck features no infinite combos. There are no Dualcaster Mage/Twinflame loops or Kiki-Jiki/Deceiver Exarch instant-wins. Instead, the deck wins via severe, overwhelming combat value or pseudo-extra-turn locks using Time Stop, Discontinuity, Glorious End, and Last Chance. Because you have to swing to win, your win conditions are inherently healthier for casual metas, but it does mean your games will rarely end before Turn 6, as you must explicitly build a board state and find an opening.
