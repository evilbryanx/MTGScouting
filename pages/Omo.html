Executive Summary

This is a Simic Everything-Counter Toolbox deck that leverages Omo, Queen of Vesuva to simultaneously unlock maximum value from hyper-specific land types (Locus, Urza's, Deserts) and disparate creature types. It wins by generating astronomical amounts of mana to cast massive X-spells, animating giant land-creatures, or swarming the board with unblockable, buffed tokens without relying on infinite combos.

Strategic Pillar Breakdown

1. Synergy & Engine
9 / 10

This is where the deck absolutely shines. Omo acts as a skeleton key for your entire list. By placing an everything counter on a land, it becomes a Locus (supercharging Cloudpost and Glimmerpost), an Urza's Mine/Tower/Power Plant/Workshop (instantly turning on the Tron package), and a Desert to enable your cycling lands or Hashep Oasis.

On the creature side, everything counters turn utility dorks into Merfolk for Hakbal of the Surging Soul, Wizards for Azami, Lady of Scrolls, and Rogues for Nimble Trapfinder. The inclusion of Thassa, Deep-Dwelling, Displacer Kitten, and Splash Portal allows you to blink Omo or other value pieces to spread everything counters across the board at an alarming rate. Roaming Throne naming "Avatar" or "Noble" doubles Omo's triggers, creating an engine that feels incredibly cohesive and explosive.

2. Interaction & Board Control
6 / 10

Your interaction suite features high-quality premium staples like Mana Drain, Fierce Guardianship, and Cyclonic Rift, which can single-handedly swing games. Spot removal like Beast Within, Pongify, and Volatile Fault gives you clean answers to problematic permanents.

The raw density of interaction is a bit low for a high-power casual meta. If Omo is removed or countered multiple times, the deck can struggle to control aggressive boards.

While Collective Restraint is a hilarious and brilliant inclusion (Omo makes your lands have all basic land types, meaning Collective Restraint easily gives you a Domain tax of 5 colorless mana per attacking creature), you could use a few more low-mana responses to protect your engine before it establishes dominance.

3. Consistency & Tutors
7 / 10

Lands-matter strategies are inherently consistent due to cards like Expedition Map, Hour of Promise, and your suite of non-basic utilities. You have top-tier card selection via Sylvan Library and massive burst draw like Finale of Revelation, Drown in Dreams, and Sea Gate Restoration.

The main vulnerability in consistency stems from the reliance on Omo to bridge your sub-themes. If you draw Cloudpost without Omo or other Locus lands, it's just a tapped land that taps for 1. If you draw tribal payoffs without Omo counters, your creatures don't talk to each other. More land-tutors that can find Vesuva or Thespian's Stage directly would push this score closer to an 8 or 9.

4. Speed & Mana Base
8 / 10

The ceiling of your mana base is terrifyingly high. A single everything counter on a random land alongside Urza's Workshop or Cloudpost can easily yield 4 to 10 mana from a single land drop. Spells like Awaken the Woods, Reshape the Earth, and Aesi, Tyrant of Gyre Strait ensure your hand stays full of fuel and your battlefield stays flooded with mana.

The primary drawback is the number of utility lands that tap for colorless or enter the battlefield tapped (like Lush Oasis, Simic Guildgate, and the cycling Deserts). In early turns, you may find yourself color-screwed or a turn behind the table's development. Balancing the hyper-explosive colorless lands with stable colored mana is your main building challenge.

5. Win Conditions & Closeness to "Infinite"
7 / 10

Per your deckbuilding rules, there are no infinite combos here. Instead, you win through overwhelming value and combat steps. Dark Depths comboed with Thespian's Stage (or just Omo turning Dark Depths into a land with no counters so it immediately sacrifices into Marit Lage) provides a massive 20/20 indestructible flier.

For wide boards, Avenger of Zendikar, Scute Swarm, and Rampaging Baloths generate armies that become lethal when paired with Herald of Hoofbeats (giving your everything-counter creatures Horsemanship) or Lord of Atlantis (giving them Islandwalk). Hydra Broodmaster acts as a brilliant mana sink to create an overwhelming army of giant hydras in a single turn.
