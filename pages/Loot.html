Executive Summary

This is a high-velocity Mono-Green Stompy and Big Mana deck built around a heavy creature-based engine. The deck utilizes explosive mana-generation tools like Gaea's Cradle, Karametra's Acolyte, and Selvala, Heart of the Wilds to out-ramp the table and deploy massive, high-impact threats. Loot, Exuberant Explorer acts as a reliable mid-game value engine, dropping lands into play and digging for additional threats or card draw. The primary path to victory is traditional combat damage, utilizing lethal over-run effects like Craterhoof Behemoth, Kamahl, Fist of Krosa, and Zopandrel, Hunger Dominus to turn a wide board of mana dorks and utility creatures into an unstoppable, lethal army in a single turn.

1. Synergy & Engine
8.5 / 10

The deck functions as a cohesive ecosystem where mana generation and card advantage are tightly interwoven. Loot, Exuberant Explorer synergizes well with the 31 Snow-Covered Forest lands, ensuring its land-drop ability hits consistently while its upkeep trigger digs deeper into the library.

  • The Draw Engines: Mass draw spells like Greater Good, Return of the Wildspeaker, Momentous Fall, and Regal Force capitalize on high-power creatures to refill your hand instantly, preventing the deck from running out of steam.
  • Creature Storm Loops: Permanent-based value engines such as The Great Henge, Tribute to the World Tree, Elemental Bond, and Beast Whisperer turn every subsequent creature spell into a self-replacing cantrip or better.
  • Implicit Synergies: Goreclaw, Terror of Qal Sisma and Defiler of Vigor act as strong enablers, lowering the structural casting cost of your high-end threats while simultaneously pumping the team.
2. Interaction & Board Control
5.0 / 10

While highly proactive, interaction is the clear weak point of this build. The deck relies almost exclusively on creature-based fight or bite mechanics and high-cost utility spells, leaving it vulnerable to fast combo strategies or non-creature permanents.

  • Targeted Removal: Kogla, the Titan Ape and Ulvenwald Tracker handle opposing creature threats effectively by leveraging your creatures' massive power stats, while Silverback Elder handles artifacts and enchantments upon casting creatures.
  • Massive Cleaners: Terastodon acts as a catch-all answer for problematic lands, planeswalkers, or artifacts, while Silklash Spider handles flying sweeps.
  • Stack/Board Protection: Heroic Intervention is your lone true piece of instant-speed stack protection against board wipes. The lack of low-cost, instant-speed interactions makes the deck vulnerable to faster, more interactive opponents.
3. Consistency & Tutors
8.0 / 10

Mono-Green excels at locating exactly what it needs, and this deck utilizes a premier selection of creature-focused tutors to guarantee consistent execution of its game plan.

  • The Tutor Suite: Green Sun's Zenith, Chord of Calling, and Natural Order allow you to cheat key utility pieces or silver-bullet answers directly into play at minimal cost.
  • Game-Enders: Tooth and Nail serves as a direct 9-mana tutor payload that wins the game on the spot by fetching lethal two-card combinations (e.g., Craterhoof Behemoth + Avenger of Zendikar).
  • Recursion: Eternal Witness and Praetor's Counsel offer powerful graveyard insurance, with the latter resetting your entire graveyard back into your hand to completely out-grind opponents in prolonged matches.
4. Speed & Mana Base
9.0 / 10

The mana base and ramp package are highly optimized, allowing the deck to jump from 3 mana to 8+ mana with incredible speed.

  • Explosive Lands: The inclusion of Gaea's Cradle, Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx, and Castle Garenbrig ensures that your land base scales exponentially based on your board presence.
  • High-Octane Acceleration: Selvala, Heart of the Wilds, Karametra's Acolyte, and Fanatic of Rhonas generate massive bursts of mana based on devotion or high power stats, which can then be funneled into X-spells like Genesis Wave.
  • Mana Doublers: Extraplanar Lens paired with Snow-Covered Forest inputs an asymmetrical doubling engine that leaves opponents behind without accelerating their mana bases.
5. Win Conditions & Closeness to Infinite
7.0 / 10

The deck relies on overwhelming combat damage rather than complex, dedicated infinite-loop combos. However, its raw damage output is so high that it functions similarly to a combo finish.

  • Overwhelming Combat: Craterhoof Behemoth paired with a developed board is the definitive finisher. Zopandrel, Hunger Dominus and Kamahl, Fist of Krosa serve as repeatable, high-damage alternatives that turn modest boards into lethal combat steps.
  • Massive Cheat Effects: Apex Devastator and Ghalta, Stampede Tyrant dump vast chunks of power onto the battlefield in a single turn, forcing immediate answers or causing swift deaths on the subsequent combat step.
  • Infinite Potential: While explicit infinite mana loops (like Ashaya lines) are absent, cards like Seedborn Muse paired with Yeva, Nature's Herald or Vedalken Orrery allow you to play completely on your opponents' turns, effectively multiplying your available mana and turns by four.
